Problem-solving skills

Software development is all about solving problems, whether they're big or small. Developers need to be able to identify problems, analyze them from different angles, and come up with creative solutions. They also need to be able to think critically and make sound decisions under pressure.

Technical skills

Developers need to have a strong understanding of computer science fundamentals, such as data structures, algorithms, and programming languages. They also need to be familiar with a variety of development tools and technologies. The specific technical skills required will vary depending on the type of software development you're doing, but it's important to have a strong foundation in the basics.

Communication skills

Software developers spend a lot of time communicating with others, including clients, project managers, other developers, and end-users. They need to be able to communicate their ideas clearly and concisely, both verbally and in writing. They also need to be able to listen effectively and understand the needs of others.
